# Slide Vocabulary

21 canonical slide types. Every slide in every deck must use one of these types. If content does not fit, flag as `UNCLASSIFIED` and resolve with Larry before proceeding.

Each entry: **Type name** — Definition. *Primary use case.* Do not use when: [constraint].

---

| # | Type | Definition | Primary use case | Do not use when |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 00 | Title | Deck opener showing topic label, main title, presenter name, and date. No body content. | First slide of every deck. | The deck has already been introduced — Title appears once only. |
| 01 | Statement | A single bold claim occupying the slide. No supporting content, no stat, no label. | Stating the PoV or a sub-claim that must land before evidence is introduced. | The claim needs evidence on the same slide — use Evidence instead. |
| 02 | Stat | One dominant number at display scale with a minimal label below. Nothing else. | Introducing a key number before its context is explained. | The number needs a source or explanation on the same slide — use Evidence. |
| 03 | Evidence | A stat or finding at large scale, plus a source citation and one sentence of context. | Backing a claim with a credible number and its provenance. | The number stands alone without context — use Stat. |
| 04 | Quote | An attributed quote in large italic type with speaker name and role. | Borrowing authority from a credible voice to support a claim. | The quote needs explanation — quotes must be self-sufficient. |
| 05 | Comparison | Two columns contrasting two states, options, or time periods. Each column has a label and a value. | Showing before/after, with/without, or A vs B. | More than two columns are needed — use List or Chart. |
| 06 | List | 3–5 discrete items, each a short phrase, with a shared claim as the slide title. | Enumerating parallel points that each stand alone. | Items have unequal weight or need sub-explanation — use Evidence or split into separate slides. |
| 07 | Image + Claim | A full-bleed or dominant generated image with a short caption claim below or overlaid. | Making a claim emotionally legible through a visual. | No image can be argued as necessary — use Statement instead. |
| 08 | Question / Hook | A rhetorical question in large type, nothing else. | Opening a section, disrupting passive listening, or prompting the audience to sit forward. | The question has an obvious answer — it must create genuine tension. |
| 09 | CTA | A closing action slide: one imperative statement and one button or URL. No other content. | The final slide — always. One ask only. | There are multiple asks — choose one. |
| 10 | Section Divider | Part/section number, an accent rule, and the section title. No body content. | Marking a structural break in a long deck. | The deck has fewer than three logical sections — dividers add ceremony without value in short decks. |
| 11 | Chart / Graph | A data visualisation (bar, line, scatter, or pie) with a title and axis labels. | Showing trends, distributions, or comparisons across more than two data points. | Two data points — use Comparison instead. |
| 12 | Timeline | A vertical or horizontal sequence of dated events with a label per event. | Showing how something evolved over time. | The sequence is not time-based — use Process / Steps instead. |
| 13 | Split (text + image) | Body copy on one side, generated image on the other. Roughly equal width. | When a claim needs both verbal explanation and visual reinforcement simultaneously. | The image can carry the slide alone — use Image + Claim instead. |
| 14 | Process / Steps | Numbered steps connected by arrows. Each step has a number and a short label. | Showing a sequence of actions where order matters. | The sequence is time-based with dates — use Timeline instead. |
| 15 | Table of Contents | Numbered agenda items, each one line, no detail. | Orienting the audience at the start of a structured deck. | The deck is fewer than five slides — a TOC adds overhead without value. |
| 16 | About / Vision | Eyebrow label, a mission or vision statement in large type, and one paragraph of supporting narrative. | Establishing organisational purpose or a founding belief. | The statement is the presenter's personal position — use Statement or About Me instead. |
| 17 | Team / People | 2–4 person cards in a row, each with an avatar, name, and role. | Introducing a group of people. | There is only one person — use About Me instead. |
| 18 | Services / Offerings | A 2×2 or 3-column grid of service cards, each with an icon, name, and one-line description. | Presenting a structured range of offerings. | There are more than 6 items — the grid becomes unreadable; use List or split into multiple slides. |
| 19 | Gallery / Work Samples | A 3-column image grid with minimal text. | Showing a portfolio of visual work. | The images require explanation — pair with Split or Evidence instead. |
| 20 | About Me | Presenter photo, name, title, short bio (2–3 sentences), and credential tags. | Introducing the presenter personally, usually early in the deck. | The slide introduces multiple people — use Team / People instead. |
