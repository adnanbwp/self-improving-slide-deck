01
Brand story

State it,
then stop.

aha agile is an editorial identity built on restraint. It treats every surface like the front page of a quiet, confident magazine: one dominant idea, set large, with everything else pushed to the margins as small monospace annotation. The mood is deadpan — dry humour delivered with a straight face.

Three things make it recognisable anywhere: a two-colour field split by a hard seam, oversized numerals that bleed off the edge, and a single heavy headline doing all the talking. Nothing is decorated that doesn't need to be. The white space is the design.

02
Logo & mark

The badge

  • → An ink disc, a bone hairline ring, the spark mark. That's the whole logo.
  • → Keep clear space of at least half the disc's diameter on every side (dashed guide).
  • → Minimum size 40px on screen. Below that, drop the inner mark and keep the ring.
  • → Place on ink or orange. On paper, keep the bone ring so the disc reads as deliberate.
  • → Never recolour the disc, stretch it, or add a drop shadow heavier than the system's.

Field rule — orange shouts, paper reads, ink breaks. Use orange for openers, statements and calls to action; paper for evidence, lists and anything text-heavy; ink sparingly, as a structural blackout. Tints stay on their own field: orange-tint only on orange, paper-tint only on paper.

05
Voice & tone

Dry, brief,
unbothered.

We do

  • State one idea, then stop
  • Use lowercase asides as a shrug
  • Let understatement carry the joke
  • Trust the reader to fill the gap

We don't

  • Use exclamation marks or hype
  • Explain a line that should land alone
  • Stack three adjectives where none will do
  • Sound like a motivational poster
Stand-ups, but shorter.headline — deadpan, says the quiet part
just a team.annotation — lowercase, dry, in mono italic
