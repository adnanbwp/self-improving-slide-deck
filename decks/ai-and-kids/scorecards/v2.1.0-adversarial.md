## Attack 1 — Slide 19 "You are the difference" vs Slide 16 honest scope

**Severity: Significant** (and its real damage is that it amplifies the standing Critical, CA-2)

**Steelman.** Slide 19 can be read as consistent with slide 16. It enumerates the *other* levers a parent holds — "Not the perfect rule, not the right app, not the ban" — and demotes them in favour of presence. On that reading "you are the difference" means "of the options you control, presence is the deciding one," not "you alone solve the whole problem, product design included." "Same product, different adult, different child" is a directional callback to slide 14, whose own note flags the claim as directional, not causal. Read charitably, 19 does not contradict 16.

**Attack.** Adversarial resilience is about the hostile reading, and the hostile reading is available and lands hard. Slide 16 spends a full dark-background beat honestly conceding that AI companion products are engineered to form attachment, that platforms fail to detect teen crises, that these are "product and policy failures that need industry action," and — per its own note — that "adult guidance is the audience's lever, not the complete solution." Three slides later the deck's terminal, emphasis-maximised, delivered-slow CTA says, flatly and in the absolute: **"You are the difference."** Not *a* difference; not *the difference you can make*. The closer is the last and loudest thing the room hears, and rhetorically the closer overwrites the mid-deck concession. This is CA-2 (sycophancy sufficiency) reborn at maximum volume: for the highest-risk child — the one who prefers the AI, who uses it specifically to avoid the adult — the product has already outcompeted the adult relationship, and "you are the difference" is exactly the overclaim, told to exactly the parent slide 16 conceded cannot be the whole answer. The deck bought humility on 16 and spends it on 19.

This is not a *logical* contradiction — the two slides can be reconciled. It is a *rhetorical-emphatic* one: the deck ends on the maximal claim at the position of greatest weight and lets the concession fade. The new CTA adds no evidence; it raises the finality and salience of an overclaim the deck's own evidence does not support for the at-risk population.

**Required resolution.** A satisfactory response must stop the terminal beat from standing as an unqualified absolute while slide 16 concedes insufficiency. It would either (a) carry a visible trace of the slide-16 scope into the close (the difference *you can make*, the lever *you hold*), or (b) make it visible on the slide — not only in the reconciling reading — that "you are the difference" is scoped to the audience's option set, not a claim of sufficiency against the engineered product. How to word it is Penn/Rex's call, not mine.

## Attack 2 — Slide 03 anecdote as the deck's emotional proof-of-concept

**Severity: Low** (bordering Significant via its reinforcement of CA-3)

**Steelman.** Slide 03 is a bio slide. Its job is ethos, not evidence. The argument's evidentiary weight is carried by cited research on slides 09–16, which stands independently of the presenter's story. An anecdote in an "About the Presenter" slot is not offered as proof and cannot fairly be attacked as anecdote-as-evidence.

**Attack.** The anecdote does not stay in its bio lane. Slide 19's note explicitly ties the entire behavioural ask back to "the same move from the presenter's own story on slide 03," and slide 03 models the exact prescribed behaviour verbatim ("do you think that's right, and how would we check?"). The deck now opens and closes on the same vignette — a bookend. That promotes the anecdote from bio-colour to the narrative frame around the whole argument. Two consequences a hostile reader will exploit:

- **n=1 with no outcome.** The deck's most vivid demonstration that "pulling up a chair works" is a single unverified self-report in which we never learn what the child concluded or whether anything changed. As emotional proof-of-concept it is a feel-good vignette, not evidence — and the bookend structure invites the audience to weight it as more than that.
- **Survivorship / archetype reinforcement of CA-3.** The presenter is, by construction, the engaged parent who pulled up the chair. The deck is now authored by, and bookended by, the exact engaged-parent archetype whose *absence* is the real problem the deck names (the 49%). This slightly worsens the standing equity attack (CA-3): the deck embodies the person it already can't reach.

**Required resolution.** No fix to the evidentiary spine is required — the research still carries the argument. What must be addressed is only the framing risk if the bookend is leaned on in delivery: the presenter's story must not be presented as evidence that presence is sufficient. This is Low because the research is insulated from it; it edges toward Significant only through the CA-3 linkage.

## Attack 3 — Slide 03 authenticity / fabrication risk for non-Adnan presenters

**Severity: Low**

The canonical file hardcodes Adnan's specific first-person moment, and the note instructs presenters to "swap in your own real moment." A presenter who did not do this and delivers the shipped line as first-person is telling a fabricated anecdote — an ethos-collapse risk if challenged ("did that actually happen?"). The note mitigates it well ("your own real moment," "what you actually did, not what you believe"), so this is a presenter-integrity risk, not a deck-argument vulnerability. Named for completeness; the mitigation is already present.

## Score rationale

Held at **6/10** against the filed 6/10 baseline. The deck banked a genuine resilience gain since v1.1.0 (CA-5 closed on slide 16). The two v2.1.0 changes are, on pure adversarial terms, net slightly negative: slide 19 relocates and sharpens the standing **Critical** (CA-2) to the loudest position in the deck without adding a shred of evidence to support the absolute it now ends on, and slide 03 marginally reinforces the equity **Significant** (CA-3). The trust and payoff gains the recut was built for are real, but ethos and narrative closure harden the deck against *casual* challenge, not against the well-prepared hostile reader the score tracks. Banked CA-5 closure roughly offsets the new slide-19 amplification of CA-2, so the number does not move. It is a fair 6 that is one honest sentence on the close away from being a 5: if the terminal overclaim is weighted as heavily as it deserves, the deck's strongest attack is now the *last* thing it says.

**Resilience: preserved.** Narratively improved, adversarially flat, with one new Significant surface (the closing overclaim) that a motivated opponent will now lead with precisely because it is the deck's final and most emphatic line.

**Attack tally (current standing):** Critical 1 · Significant 4 · Low 4 · Resolved 1.

**Single strongest attack:** The closing "You are the difference," delivered as the deck's terminal beat, rhetorically overwrites slide 16's honest concession that adult guidance is insufficient against adversarially engineered products — reviving the sycophancy-sufficiency Critical at maximum emphasis and zero added evidence.
