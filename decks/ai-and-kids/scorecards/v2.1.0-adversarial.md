## Attack 1 — Slide 19 "You are the difference" vs Slide 16 honest scope

**Severity: Significant** (RESOLVED at re-score — see "Re-score after fix" below)

**Steelman.** Slide 19 can be read as consistent with slide 16. It enumerates the *other* levers a parent holds — "Not the perfect rule, not the right app, not the ban" — and demotes them in favour of presence. On that reading "you are the difference" means "of the options you control, presence is the deciding one," not "you alone solve the whole problem, product design included." "Same product, different adult, different child" is a directional callback to slide 14, whose own note flags the claim as directional, not causal. Read charitably, 19 does not contradict 16.

**Attack.** Adversarial resilience is about the hostile reading, and the hostile reading is available and lands hard. Slide 16 spends a full dark-background beat honestly conceding that AI companion products are engineered to form attachment, that platforms fail to detect teen crises, that these are "product and policy failures that need industry action," and — per its own note — that "adult guidance is the audience's lever, not the complete solution." Three slides later the deck's terminal, emphasis-maximised, delivered-slow CTA said, flatly and in the absolute: **"You are the difference."** Not *a* difference; not *the difference you can make*. The closer is the last and loudest thing the room hears, and rhetorically the closer overwrites the mid-deck concession. This is CA-2 (sycophancy sufficiency) reborn at maximum volume: for the highest-risk child — the one who prefers the AI, who uses it specifically to avoid the adult — the product has already outcompeted the adult relationship, and "you are the difference" is exactly the overclaim, told to exactly the parent slide 16 conceded cannot be the whole answer. The deck bought humility on 16 and spent it on 19.

This was not a *logical* contradiction — the two slides could be reconciled. It was a *rhetorical-emphatic* one: the deck ended on the maximal claim at the position of greatest weight and let the concession fade. The original CTA added no evidence; it raised the finality and salience of an overclaim the deck's own evidence does not support for the at-risk population.

**Required resolution.** A satisfactory response must stop the terminal beat from standing as an unqualified absolute while slide 16 concedes insufficiency. It would either (a) carry a visible trace of the slide-16 scope into the close (the difference *you can make*, the lever *you hold*), or (b) make it visible on the slide — not only in the reconciling reading — that "you are the difference" is scoped to the audience's option set, not a claim of sufficiency against the engineered product.

## Attack 2 — Slide 03 anecdote as the deck's emotional proof-of-concept

**Severity: Low** (bordering Significant via its reinforcement of CA-3)

**Steelman.** Slide 03 is a bio slide. Its job is ethos, not evidence. The argument's evidentiary weight is carried by cited research on slides 09–16, which stands independently of the presenter's story. An anecdote in an "About the Presenter" slot is not offered as proof and cannot fairly be attacked as anecdote-as-evidence.

**Attack.** The anecdote does not stay in its bio lane. Slide 19's note explicitly ties the entire behavioural ask back to "the same move from the presenter's own story on slide 03," and slide 03 models the exact prescribed behaviour verbatim ("do you think that's right, and how would we check?"). The deck now opens and closes on the same vignette — a bookend. That promotes the anecdote from bio-colour to the narrative frame around the whole argument. Two consequences a hostile reader will exploit:

- **n=1 with no outcome.** The deck's most vivid demonstration that "pulling up a chair works" is a single unverified self-report in which we never learn what the child concluded or whether anything changed. As emotional proof-of-concept it is a feel-good vignette, not evidence — and the bookend structure invites the audience to weight it as more than that.
- **Survivorship / archetype reinforcement of CA-3.** The presenter is, by construction, the engaged parent who pulled up the chair. The deck is now authored by, and bookended by, the exact engaged-parent archetype whose *absence* is the real problem the deck names (the 49%). This slightly worsens the standing equity attack (CA-3): the deck embodies the person it already can't reach.

**Required resolution.** No fix to the evidentiary spine is required — the research still carries the argument. What must be addressed is only the framing risk if the bookend is leaned on in delivery: the presenter's story must not be presented as evidence that presence is sufficient. This is Low because the research is insulated from it; it edges toward Significant only through the CA-3 linkage.

## Attack 3 — Slide 03 authenticity / fabrication risk for non-Adnan presenters

**Severity: Low**

The canonical file hardcodes Adnan's specific first-person moment, and the note instructs presenters to "swap in your own real moment." A presenter who did not do this and delivers the shipped line as first-person is telling a fabricated anecdote — an ethos-collapse risk if challenged ("did that actually happen?"). The note mitigates it well ("your own real moment," "what you actually did, not what you believe"), so this is a presenter-integrity risk, not a deck-argument vulnerability. Named for completeness; the mitigation is already present.

## Re-score after fix (2026-07-02)

**Revised score: 7/10. Resilience: Improved (+1 vs the 6/10 baseline).**

Larry applied my prescribed fix to slide 19. The support line now reads:

> "Not the perfect rule. Not the right app. Not the ban. **Those fights are real, and they'll take years. The adult who pulls up a chair this week is the part that's yours today.** Same product, different adult, different child."

(Imperative "You are the difference." and the button unchanged.)

**Verdict on Attack 1: RESOLVED.** This carries slide 16's exact two-part scope onto the CTA, directly beneath the imperative:

- **"Those fights are real, and they'll take years"** reinstates the industry-action / regulatory-timeline concession from slide 16. The close now concedes, at the terminal beat, that the product/policy fights are real and slow — i.e., that presence is *not* the whole answer. That is the opposite of an overwrite.
- **"The part that's yours today"** explicitly bounds "you are the difference" to the audience's option set rather than to sufficiency against the engineered product. This is resolution path (b) made visible on the slide, not left to the charitable reading.

The four-word absolute no longer floats free. It is qualified on its own slide and now *mirrors* slide 16's structure (real fights / years vs your lever / this week) — 19 has become a callback to the honest-scope beat instead of a contradiction of it. A hostile reader who tries to isolate "You are the difference" as an overclaim is immediately met, on the same slide, by "the part that's yours today," which pre-empts the sufficiency reading. The terminal-beat re-amplification of the CA-2 Critical is removed: the close no longer tells the at-risk parent that presence is sufficient — it says the harder fights exist and will take years, and the chair is the part they own now.

**Effect on the standing Critical (CA-2).** Not resolved — and it was never claimed to be by this fix. CA-2 (no evidence that adult-mediated relationships are resilient against engineered attachment for the highest-risk child) returns to its *standing* exposure. The fix removes the *added* exposure the close created; it does not close the underlying content gap. That, and the unverified 49% / 96% / 11% statistics (CA-4), remain next-cycle content work and are the two reasons the score is capped at 7 rather than 8.

**Why +1.** Relative to the filed 6/10: the deck banked the CA-5 closure (the single most-repeated "required resolution" across prior scorecards — the scope justification), the v2.1.0 cycle added a trust beat (03) and a payoff close (19) that now carries scope rather than overwriting it, and the one net-negative the additive changes introduced (the slide-19 amplification) has been neutralised. The additive cycle is now net positive on adversarial resilience with no lasting new vulnerability. A 7 states: resilient against a well-prepared hostile reader on structure and scope; still exposed on two specific unresolved content items.
