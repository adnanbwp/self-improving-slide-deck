**Score:** 8/10

## Summary

The v1.0.0 deck represents a disciplined execution of the revised argument map. It addresses all four prior significant gaps from the v1 audit, closes the two new gaps introduced by the supplement, and names the evidence asymmetry explicitly rather than papering over it — which is exactly the honest handling the argument required. The structural architecture is strengthened by integrating the digital native myth as Warrant Zero (Gap 7 repair) and by adopting the Google AI Overviews impossibility argument as the primary Limb A warrant. The score does not reach 9 because two weaknesses remain: the 49%/96%/11% statistics are presented with a confidence caveat (Medium-High) that is appropriate but unresolved, and the bridge from Limb B to Limb C (why adult guidance is still the answer despite adversarial product design) is implied rather than argued directly.

## Gap Coverage

**Gap 1 — Evidence asymmetry between Limb A and Limb B**
Status: **Closed.**
Slide 5 explicitly states: "The harms here are structural and inferential — real but of a different order than the acute harms documented in Limb B." Slide 11 dedicates a full evidence asymmetry disclosure slide naming the difference in kind between the two limbs and framing the acute hands-off evidence as a reason for urgency rather than a reason to dismiss the structural harm of restriction. This is exactly the required fix from the argument map and it is executed precisely.

**Gap 2 — Adult AI literacy precondition is unmet and unaddressed**
Status: **Substantially closed, with one residual caveat.**
Slide 13 addresses this directly and forcefully. It opens with the 49% statistic, names the 96% school-policy figure and the 11% teacher-training figure, explicitly states "this deck is not written for the expert co-pilot parent," and reframes the required behaviors as existing skills applied to a new context. The actionable calibration — "one conversation, or one question asked aloud" — speaks to the actual starting point of the 49%, not the ideal parent. The residual caveat: the statistics are presented as Medium-High confidence via practitioner newsletter, and the in-slide note correctly flags this. If those figures do not hold against the primary Common Sense Media 2026 report, the evidentiary weight of this slide drops significantly. The gap is structurally closed but statistically provisional.

**Gap 3 — Product design counter-argument unacknowledged**
Status: **Closed.**
Slide 8 names the sycophancy mechanism explicitly as design intent, not side effect, sourced to APA Monitor October 2025. Slide 14 then provides a dedicated "honest scope" slide that acknowledges product design and policy failures, scopes the deck's guidance to the audience's lever, and explicitly states that adult guidance "operates within a product environment that is, in some cases, actively working against the outcomes you want for your child." The non-sequitur risk from the argument map is directly addressed. The logical bridge — "the reason adult presence matters more, not less, is because the product is adversarial" — appears in the slide 14 speaker notes and is partially surfaced in the slide body. This is a clean repair.

**Gap 4 — Haidt analogy as evidence rather than rhetorical framing**
Status: **Closed by omission — Haidt does not appear in the deck.**
The Google AI Overviews impossibility argument has displaced the Haidt analogy as the Limb A anchor entirely. Haidt is not named anywhere in the 17 slides. The required fix was to demote Haidt to rhetorical framing only; Coda instead chose not to use it, which is the more conservative and defensible resolution. No residual weakness here.

**Gap 5 — Age-scoping absent from PoV**
Status: **Closed.**
Slide 6 is entirely dedicated to this qualifier. It distinguishes blanket avoidance from age-appropriate graduated limits, endorses the Common Sense Media thresholds verbatim (no AI companions under 5; caution 6–12; no social AI companion apps under 18), and explicitly states: "age-appropriate restriction within a framework of guided exposure is not the problem." The slide appears before Limb B, which is the correct placement — the audience needs this qualifier before encountering the harm evidence to avoid misreading the deck as opposing all restriction.

**Gap 6 — Guidance efficacy asserted, not evidenced**
Status: **Closed.**
Slide 12 carries a clearly visible evidence note: "Guidance efficacy is directionally well-evidenced; no RCTs for AI parenting interventions at scale yet exist. The claim is that this approach is the right direction — not that its effect size is proven." The qualifier is calibrated: it does not undercut the guidance claim, it accurately represents the evidentiary state.

**Gap 7 — Digital native myth not structurally integrated**
Status: **Closed.**
Slide 2 is entirely structured around this repair. The digital native assumption is named as "the engine behind both failures," appearing before the three-schools orientation and before either limb's evidence. It functions as Warrant Zero — the single shared false premise that both failure modes express. This tightens the argument architecture precisely as the argument map recommended.

**Gap 8 — Section 5 (AI companionship substitution) stranded without actionable guidance**
Status: **Closed, using the recommended approach.**
Slide 15 deploys the Child Mind Institute warning signs checklist and adds a final sentence: "These signals apply to AI over-dependence generally — including in cases where a child's peer social channels have been restricted and AI may be filling that social role." This follows the argument map's Option (c) — use the Section 5 material as reinforcing context for the general warning signs slide. The substitution hypothesis is not overstated; the checklist is presented as applicable to the general case, with the substitution scenario acknowledged as a subset.

**Gap 9 — Three-schools framing requires Gap 5 resolution first**
Status: **Closed.**
Slide 3 adopts the three-schools framing and explicitly states: "School 1 is necessary but not sufficient." The slide names that School 1 "is right about a lot — some restrictions at specific ages are supported by the evidence" before calling it insufficient as a comprehensive strategy. Slide 6 then closes Gap 5. The sequencing is correct: three-schools framing on Slide 3, age-scoping qualifier on Slide 6, before Limb B's harm evidence begins on Slide 7.

## Remaining Weaknesses

**Weakness 1 — Statistical foundation of Gap 2 repair is provisionally sourced (Significant)**
The 49%/96%/11% statistics are the evidentiary backbone of Slide 13's adult unpreparedness argument — the slide's entire framing depends on them being accurate. The in-slide caveat ("Medium-High confidence — verify against primary reports before live presentation") is epistemically correct and appropriately disclosed. However, this means the deck cannot be presented in its current form without completing the verification step. The Common Sense Media 2026 primary report verification is an open task flagged to Coda in decisions.md. Until that verification is complete, the deck's strongest Gap 2 repair rests on unconfirmed figures. This is not a logical flaw in the argument — it is an evidentiary dependency that must be resolved before production use.

**Weakness 2 — The B-to-C bridge is implied in the notes but thin on the slide face (Minor)**
The transition from Limb B (products are adversarially designed against children) to Limb C (adult guidance is the answer) is the structural non-sequitur risk identified in the argument map. Slide 14 closes Gap 3 by acknowledging the product design problem and scoping the audience's lever. However, the explicit logical bridge — "because the product is engineered to form attachment, the presence of an adult asking questions is the specific countervailing force that disrupts that attachment cycle" — appears in the speaker notes for Slide 14 but is stated more obliquely on the slide face: "Acknowledging that is not defeatism. It is accuracy — and it is the reason the guidance in this deck matters more, not less." A sophisticated audience reading slides without notes may still find the jump from "the product is adversarial" to "here is what you do" underexplained. The notes carry the load; the slide body does not fully close it.

**Weakness 3 — Lancet Digital Health article unresolved (Minor)**
The Lancet Digital Health article on Australia's social media ban effects (HTTP 403 in Pax's sweep) remains unaccessed. Decisions.md flags this as an open item for Coda. If that article documents adverse mental health outcomes from the ban, Section 5's AI companion substitution concern would move from a plausible inference to a partially confirmed finding, which would affect the framing of Slide 15. The current framing is defensible given available evidence; this is a forward-looking gap, not a current logical flaw.

## Warrant Assessment

**Limb A — Impossibility warrant (Slide 4):** Strongly warranted. The Google AI Overviews factual claim is sourced to Search Engine Land (2024), The Conversation (May 2026), and Internet Matters (2025). The argument from factual impossibility ("the decision has already been made for you — by Google") is logically stronger than the prior normative harm argument and the sourcing is specific. The warrant holds on its own terms.

**Limb A — Equity/literacy warrant (Slide 5):** Adequately warranted. Brookings (2024) supports the school ban ineffectiveness claim; OECD/EC (May 2025) supports the international competency baseline claim. The evidence asymmetry acknowledgment on Slide 5 is correctly proportionate. Warrant holds.

**Limb B — Sycophancy mechanism (Slide 8):** Warranted with appropriate precision. The claim attributes design intent to APA Monitor (October 2025) citing media psychologist Don Grant, corroborated by Common Sense Media/Stanford testing. The language "purposely engineered" is justified by the cited source. The claim does not overstate: it names the mechanism, identifies the vulnerable population, and draws the logical consequence. Warrant holds.

**Limb B — Documented harm (Slide 9):** Warranted. The Character.AI case is correctly presented as corroborating a pattern, not as standalone proof. The three-source structure (single case + systematic testing + population-scale usage data) is the right evidentiary architecture for this claim. Warrant holds.

**Limb C — Adult mediation as the variable (Slide 12):** Adequately warranted with the correct qualification. Springer Nature (2025), UNESCO (2024), and the UK NLT (2025) are cited as converging institutional evidence. The RCT caveat is present and correctly placed. The directional claim ("this is the right approach") is supported; the efficacy claim ("its effect size is proven") is deliberately withheld. Warrant holds.

**Limb C — Actionable behaviors (Slides 13, 16):** Warranted for the behaviors themselves; provisionally warranted for the unpreparedness statistics. APA (2025), Penn State Extension (2024/2025), and Common Sense Media (2025) back the specific behavioral guidance. The 49%/96%/11% statistics are appropriately flagged as Medium-High confidence pending primary source verification. The logical structure of the claim does not depend on those exact figures — even if they shift, the direction (most adults are underprepared) is well-supported across the literature. The specific numbers are impact amplifiers; the warrant does not collapse without them.
