## Audit of the two additive changes

### Slide 03 — trust anecdote (ethos, not evidence)
No unwarranted load-bearing claim. The bio narrates the presenter's own behavior and makes no
efficacy claim — it never asserts a result. It introduces no new Claim into the argument chain;
the deck's conclusion does not rest on it. It models the slide-18 item-1 recommended behavior and
illustrates the slide-06 Google-homework factual anchor without overreaching. Warrant intact.
Minor positive: it gives the CTA's "one conversation" a concrete referent.

### Slide 19 — closing payoff ("You are the difference")
Does not break the argument and is consistent with honest-scope slide 16. "You are the difference"
restates the deck's existing central claim (slide 04 "adult presence is the deciding variable";
slide 14 "different adult, different outcome") and adds no claim beyond what the limbs already warrant.
The support line ("Not the perfect rule. Not the right app. Not the ban.") reinforces slide 16's
"use the lever you have" rather than contradicting it — it directs focus to adult presence over
tools/rules/bans and does not claim adult presence is the complete fix. One Minor watch: the callback
compresses "different outcome" -> "different child" under an unqualified "You are the difference,"
pushing a directional, non-RCT-proven warrant to the edge of overstatement. It stays in bounds because
it re-lands an existing claim, its own support disclaims the alternatives, and slide 16 stands upstream
unretracted. This is rhetorical intensity (Aria's domain), not a logical gap.
