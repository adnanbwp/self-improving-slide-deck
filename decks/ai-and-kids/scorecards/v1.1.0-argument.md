## Remaining Weaknesses

**Weakness 1 — Statistical foundation of Gap 2 repair is provisionally sourced (Significant)**
The 49%, 96%, and 11% statistics remain the evidentiary backbone of Slide 13. In v1.1.0, the 49% figure now carries an in-slide attribution to "Common Sense Media, 2024." This is an improvement in transparency over v1.0.0 but does not resolve the underlying verification gap. The attribution names the source organisation but does not specify which Common Sense Media report or confirm that the September 2024 primary report has been consulted directly. The 96% USC/CARE figure and the 11% CDT figure remain without in-slide attribution on the slide face (speaker notes only). The decisions log records verification as an open task passed to Coda; it does not record completion. This weakness is Significant, not Fatal, because the argument's logical structure does not collapse if the specific percentages shift — the directional claim is well-documented across the literature — but the specific figures are used as the primary hook on a key slide and their accuracy matters to the audience's trust. This must be resolved before live presentation.

**Weakness 2 — The B-to-C bridge is now partially surfaced on the slide face but not fully closed (Minor)**
In v1.1.0, Slide 4 now contains as visible body text: "The question has already shifted: not should my child use AI — but was I there when they first did?" This is the Limb A-to-C bridge in explicit form: the impossibility of over-protection converts into the urgency of presence. This is a meaningful improvement over v1.0.0. However, the specific bridge between Limb B (product is adversarially designed) and Limb C (adult guidance is the answer) — the more structurally demanding logical step — remains carried primarily by Slide 14's speaker notes. The slide body on Slide 14 states: "Acknowledging its limits makes it more credible, not less." This is a credibility argument, not a logical bridge. The precise causal mechanism — "because the product is engineered to form attachment, adult questioning is the countervailing force that disrupts that cycle" — does not appear on any slide face. A sophisticated audience reading slides without notes may still find the jump from Slide 8 (adversarial design) through to Slide 12 (guided use as the variable) underexplained. The weakness is reduced in severity by the Slide 4 improvement but not eliminated. It remains Minor.

**Weakness 3 — Lancet Digital Health article unresolved (Minor)**
