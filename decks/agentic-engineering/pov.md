# POV — Agentic Engineering "Both Axes" base deck

**Stage:** SHAPE (narrative spine filed; deck not yet authored by Coda).
**Design spec:** `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-07-03-agentic-engineering-base-deck-design.md`
**Narrative spine:** `decks/agentic-engineering/narrative-spine.md`

## Audience (builder-anchored — the canonical/base reader)

A practitioner who wants to move *their own work* vibe → agentic → production. Not a
compliance officer, not an exec buying a platform. Governance must land as **engineering
discipline** ("can I see it, stop it, own it?"), never as compliance theatre.

Real distribution within the segment (not a flat archetype):
- **The dial-chaser** — already sold on agentic; believes a passing eval = shippable. Highest
  activation gap on the *second* axis; lowest on the first. The primary reader.
- **The burned operator** — has had an agent do something surprising in prod; already senses
  the second axis but has no name or ladder for it. Wants vocabulary and a sequence.
- **The skeptic-of-process** — hears "governance" and braces for bureaucracy that slows the
  build. Must be won with the framing that governance is what *lets* them ship faster and safer.

**JTBD:** "Help me take the agent I just built and actually *deploy* it without it becoming a
silent liability." **Current belief state:** build axis = the whole journey. **Target belief:**
build axis earns the output; governance earns the deployment; you climb both.

## The one-line claim

> Agentic engineering is only half the journey. The build dial earns you a great **output**;
> **governance** — VERDICT + the maturity ladder — is what earns you a **deployment**. Climb both
> axes, or ship a perfectly-tested time-bomb.

## The CTA (single driven action — anchored on slide 18, echoed on slide 20)

**Before your next agentic task ships, EXPOSE it.** You can't govern (BIND / ENFORCE) what you
can't see, so the universal first move is the same at every altitude:

- **🧑‍💻 IC:** On your next task, add the agent to your `AGENTS.md` / harness config — name it, its
  permissions, and *you* as owner — then log its actions somewhere you can actually watch them.
- **👥 Team:** Stand up a shared agent registry — one row per agent (capability · permissions ·
  owner · last-seen). Make "no unregistered agents" the norm.
- **🏢 Org:** Fund observability *before* autonomy — no agent gets broader permissions until it is
  on the map (L2 Observed) with a named owner.

Test of a valid CTA: it is a verb the reader can do Monday. "Consider governance" fails this;
"EXPOSE your agents this week" passes.

- **Persuasion score (projected): 8 / 10** · **Audience fit: 8** · **CTA clarity: 9** — builder

  critical framing adjustment: **pre-empt the "governance = bureaucracy" friction in Act 0 and
  hold it all deck** — every governance beat must read as an engineering question (see / stop /
  own), never a policy ask, or the skeptic-of-process reader disengages before the payoff.
