## Gap register (carried from Rex's argument map)

Two fatal gaps inform structural decisions in this plan:

**G1 (FATAL):** "Consistently outperform" is a universal quantifier that Malone et al. directly refutes. This plan resolves G1 by framing the performance claim as conditional from the first time it appears (Slide 5) and by surfacing Malone explicitly on Slide 6 rather than burying the counter-evidence.

**G2 (FATAL):** The dissolution claim has no IM/DM-specific AI causation evidence. This plan resolves G2 by framing Wave 2 as intensification of documented Wave 1 pressure (which has multi-source support: Wolpers enrollment data, Cessan QE/QT mechanism, Scrum Alliance 18% dedicated-role demand, Scaled Agile institutional response) — not as a direct causal claim that AI is already eliminating IM/DM roles. The audience lived Wave 1; the deck names it and argues Wave 2 raises the same structural stakes.

**G4 (SIGNIFICANT):** The role-fragmentation counter-claim (IM/DM dissolves into tech lead + PM + AI specialist rather than evolving) is not addressed in the research brief. This plan addresses it explicitly on Slide 14 as the honest fork-in-the-road before the "new accountability" section.

## Structural notes for the HTML author

**Slide count:** 27 slides (20 main narrative + 6 appendix + 1 Team). Slides 21–27 are post-CTA and not part of the main presentation flow.

**Arc peaks:**
- Emotional peak: Slide 10 (Statement — the audience recognition moment).
- Intellectual peak: Slide 13 (the generate/decide boundary — the deck's conceptual hinge).
- Urgency peak: Slide 17 (the $110K token cost number — largest display stat in the deck).

**Dominant visual focal points per slide:**
- Slides 2, 10: single bold claim, full-bleed type.
- Slides 3, 11, 13, 14: two-column comparison with a single-sentence label at the bottom.
- Slides 5, 7, 12, 17: one dominant number at display scale.
- Slide 6: Malone finding as the dominant number, with the reconciliation sentence below.
- Slides 8, 15, 16, 18: claim title + 3–5 short phrases (no bullet list format — each item as a standalone block).
- Slide 9: three-node timeline, horizontal.
- Slide 4: large rhetorical question, nothing else.
- Slide 19: three numbered steps, horizontal.
- Slide 20: single imperative at display scale + one URL.

**Gap handling in speaker notes (mandatory):**
- Slide 5: speaker note must state the BCG/P&G evidence applies to generative knowledge work; the claim is conditional, not universal.
- Slide 6: speaker note must explain why Malone is being surfaced proactively — it is a credibility signal for an analytically oriented audience, not a concession.
- Slide 14: speaker note must acknowledge the fragmentation path is real and that the deck's argument for evolution is probabilistic, not guaranteed.
- Slide 17: speaker note must flag that the $110K figure is a practitioner illustration based on API pricing math, not a peer-reviewed empirical study.
- Slide 18: speaker note must flag that VERDICT is a practitioner/consultant framework, not academic research — useful heuristic, not a citable study.

**Evidence confidence grades to preserve in speaker notes:**
- High: BCG RCT, P&G RCT, Malone meta-analysis, McKinsey MGI, Wolpers enrollment data, Singapore IMDA framework, Scaled Agile RTE certification update.
- Medium-High: AI4Agile Practitioners Report 2026 (n=289), Cessan QE/QT mechanism, Digital.ai State of Agile (n=350).
- Medium: Webframp collision points, VERDICT framework, token cost figures, ceremony redesign prescriptions.

**What the speaker notes must not do:**
- Repeat the slide body verbatim.
- Claim evidence certainty the research brief does not support.
- Omit the conditional qualifiers on Slides 5 and 6.
