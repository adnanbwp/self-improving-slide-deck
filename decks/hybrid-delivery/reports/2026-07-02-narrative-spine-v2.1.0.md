# Narrative Spine — hybrid-delivery v2.1.0

**Produced by:** Aria (via Larry, `/tactics` SHAPE)
**Date:** 2026-07-02
**Drives:** the v2.1.0 recut, acting on the four FAILs in `scorecards/v2.0.0-narrative.md`.

## Mode: Shape

## Objective
Convince Iteration/Delivery Managers to own what agents generate but can't decide — the governance of the human-AI boundary — and run a squad audit this week.

## Purpose mapping
Convince + Motivate — recipe: That's Funny → Data Detectives → Trust Me I'm an Expert → Hero & Guide, then Dragon & the City → No Easy Way for the leap. Rationale: the audience already has the facts around them (agents in their squad); the job is to make them back a judgment (Convince) and take a leap into owning the boundary (Motivate).

## Arc: Man in a Hole
The IM is in a comfortable place, gets knocked into the hole by the coordination gap and the bypass threat, finds the treasure in the dark (the generate/decide boundary is theirs to own), climbs out by governing it, and ends in a better place where the role has evolved. This was the *latent* arc in v2.0.0 — v2.1.0 makes it the spine instead of burying it under stacked frameworks.

## Hook
Relatable + unexpected — open on the coordination pain, not the definition of "agent": *"Your engineers are already running agents. Your PR review time is up 441% — and nobody's tracking why."*

## Beats

### Beat 1 — Comfort zone
- Purpose: Show the IM their squad as it is today, and the quiet gap opening in it.
- Deck prompts: What's the "before"? What's going unnoticed? (Slides: 441% stake → locate-yourself)
- Style reminder: Lead with the number they can check in their own sprint data.

### Beat 2 — Trigger
- Purpose: Name what's knocking them down — agents that act, climbing a capability ladder faster than the coordination around them.
- Deck prompts: What changed? Is it bad luck or not paying attention? (Slides: agent=acts → autonomy ladder → the loop)
- Style reminder: Teach what an agent is only now that they care — fast, one loop diagram, no second mechanism slide on the main line.

### Beat 3 — Crisis, treasure in the dark
- Purpose: Show the presenter's own boundary-ownership scar, then the honest evidence and the structural gap where the role gets bypassed.
- Deck prompts: What is learned/found here? (Slides: first-person trust beat → pivot question → upside + conditional evidence → bolt-on → generating-vs-governing → governance gap → the boundary)
- Style reminder: The first-person beat is the trust turn — judgment shown, not cited. Keep the evidence to one upside + one conditional.

### Beat 4 — Recovery, the climb
- Purpose: Hand them the model and mechanism for owning the boundary, and force the choice.
- Deck prompts: What changes? (Slides: centaur → HITL patterns → the fork = the crisis choice → three capabilities → VERDICT → failure modes)
- Style reminder: The fork is the emotional pivot of the whole deck — evolve or dissolve. Everything after it is the climb.

### Beat 5 — Better place
- Purpose: Show the new normal — the role evolved, governance moving with the engineers, and the one action that starts it this week.
- Deck prompts: What's the new normal? Harder to knock down next time? (Slides: squad audit → 12-month path → CTA)
- Style reminder: End on the lever they hold, not a summary. Protect the payoff line.
