## Adversarial Score: 6.5 / 10 (flat)

**Rationale:** v1.5.0 is a tighter, more honest deck — it removes one Significant attack surface (the Wave-1→Wave-2 bypass analogy) and improves several disclosures (VERDICT now attributed on-slide as a practitioner framework; Slide 14 population made explicit; Fork names the IM-vs-tech-lead contest openly). But the **two Critical attacks from v1.1.0 are untouched**, and the reorder that front-loads "your engineers are already running agents" (now Slide 6) arguably hands the tech-lead opponent their opening line *earlier* in the deck. A **new Significant attack** is introduced: VERDICT single-source dependency now underpins two slides. Improvements and new exposures roughly cancel; the unaddressed Criticals cap the score at 6.5.

## Severity-ranked attack log (v1.5.0)

**A1 — Governance accountabilities are decision tasks; the deck's own Malone evidence says human+AI degrades there. (Critical — persists, unaddressed.)** Still the single strongest attack. HITL pattern selection, approval-gate and escalation-threshold design are decision tasks; Slide 11 (Malone) establishes AI degrades human decision performance. The deck never reconciles this. The VERDICT rewrite (Slide 21) actually *adds* governance-decision surface without addressing the conflict.

**A2 — The tech lead already owns governance de facto; the deck asserts IM standing without evidence IMs win this contest. (Critical — persists; arguably sharpened.)** Moving the squad-gap forward to Slide 6 means the deck now *opens* by establishing that engineers are ahead and already running agents — which is the opponent's premise. The rebuilt Fork (Slide 18) names the contest in visible text (an improvement over notes-only), but naming the contest is not evidence of winning it.

**A3 — The governance incidents are engineering-context failures, not delivery-management failures. (Significant — persists.)** Failure-modes slide carried forward; scope qualifier still notes-only on the slide body.

**A4 — Bypass-by-Wave-1-analogy. (was Significant → DISSOLVED.)** The Wave narrative is gone, so the contested two-mechanism analogy and the "no IM-specific Wave 2 evidence" attack no longer have a target. Residual: a bare "bypass" assertion on Slide 13 → downgraded to **Low**.

**A5 — Slide 6 (squad gap) misattributes IM-vs-engineer differential to AI4Agile within-survey segmentation it does not contain. (Significant — persists.)** Slide carried forward unchanged; the qualifier ("assembled across two surveys, magnitude not single-source") is still absent from the slide body.

**A6 — Centaur chess→delivery-management transfer assumed, not demonstrated. (Low — persists.)**

**A7 — "The jobs question is settled" retains rhetorical ambiguity. (Low — persists.)**

**A8 — VERDICT single-source dependency. (Significant — NEW.)** Slides 21 (ceremonies) and 23 (governance maturity) now both rest on one practitioner framework (Srivastav/Saxena) coupled to a paid cohort, with no independent validation. Mitigated by honest on-slide attribution and by the independently-documented incidents (Knight Capital, Replit, Meta) the pillars explain — but a prepared opponent will note that two slides wobble together if VERDICT is challenged.

## Addendum (2026-06-07) — post-audit fix applied

The highest-leverage fix above was applied to the shipped v1.5.0. Slide 17 now carries, in visible body text:

> "This is workflow design — set before the agent runs. That's architecture, not the in-task judgment calls where adding AI degrades the decision."

**Effect on A1 (Malone contradiction): downgraded Critical → Significant.** The deck now explicitly distinguishes the IM's governance work (workflow design, set before execution) from the in-task AI-augmented decisions where Malone's degradation applies. The reconciliation is asserted, not yet evidenced — a residual Significant attack remains (an opponent can still demand proof that pattern-selection is genuinely design-grade and not a disguised decision task) — but the internal contradiction the deck previously ignored is now answered on the face of the slide.

**Revised count: 1 Critical / 4 Significant / 3 Low. Revised adversarial score: 7.0 / 10.**
A2 (tech-lead de facto ownership) is now the sole remaining Critical and the top target for the next cycle.
