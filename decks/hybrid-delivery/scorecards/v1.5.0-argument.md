## Assessment of the v1.5.0 changes

**1. Wave 1/2 removal — net positive for argument integrity.** The deck previously leaned on the Wolpers Scrum-Master enrollment collapse as an analogy for IM bypass in Wave 2 — an analogy Rex (v1.1.0) and Vera both flagged because no IM-specific Wave 2 evidence exists. Cutting it removes a Significant gap (prior G2 residual) and a contested two-mechanism analogy. The structural case now stands on its own chain: maturity ladder → generate/decide boundary → HITL mechanism → VERDICT cadence. *Residual (new Minor):* the bare "where the IM gets bypassed" line on Slide 13 now lacks even its analogical support; it reads as assertion. It should be framed as a forward risk, not a stated fact.

**2. New Slide 3 (Agent Capability Maturity) — earns its place.** It supplies the premise the rest of the deck depends on (the human role escalates from operator → governor as autonomy rises) and pre-arms Slide 15 (boundary) and Slide 17 (HITL). Labelled an explicit synthesis with the UW/CSA/SAE grounding in notes — honest about its evidentiary status. Strengthens the foundation of the argument chain.

**3. VERDICT rewrite (Slide 21) — closes prior N-B.** Ceremony redesigns now derive from a named governance model mapped to specific cadences, rather than free-floating prescriptions. The framework is single-source practitioner material (Srivastav/Saxena) — acceptable as an *actionable scaffold* and attributed honestly on-slide; this is a confidence flag for Vera, not a logical gap.

**4. Fork rebuilt, PM line removed — improves prior N-C and helps G-NEW-1.** The fragmentation path is now two clean items (tech lead → workflow; AI-governance specialist → risk), and the evolution-vs-fragmentation contrast puts the IM-over-tech-lead question into *visible* text for the first time — partially discharging G-NEW-1, which was previously notes-only.

**5. Locate-yourself moved early + softened.** Removing the forward-references (HITL/ceremonies/governance named before they're taught) resolves the forward-reference risk. It also removed that slide's prior visible HITL-selector cross-ref; the Fork now carries that warrant load instead. Net neutral for G-NEW-1.
