## 1. What World-Class Adversarial Critics Actually Do

The best adversarial critics operate across four established disciplines, each with distinct methods:

**Red Teaming (military/intelligence origin, adopted by AI safety and corporate strategy):** A dedicated team assumes the role of an intelligent, motivated opponent and attempts to defeat the primary team's plan or argument. The CIA's red cell and RAND Corporation's adversarial war-gaming formalized this. Day-to-day, a red teamer identifies the most damaging attack vector, not just any attack — selectivity is what separates red teaming from brainstorming objections.

**Structured Analytic Techniques (SATs) — specifically Analysis of Competing Hypotheses (ACH) and Team A/Team B analysis:** Intelligence community standard (Heuer, *Psychology of Intelligence Analysis*, 1999). ACH forces the analyst to list hypotheses, then find the evidence most *inconsistent* with each — the adversarial move is looking for disconfirming evidence rather than confirming evidence.

**Pre-Mortem (Gary Klein, 1989):** Assume the project/argument has already failed catastrophically. Work backward to explain why. The discipline is in the specificity — "it failed because X" not "it might fail because things go wrong."

**Steelmanning:** The inverse of strawmanning. Before attacking, reconstruct the strongest possible version of the opposing argument. A world-class adversarial critic steelmans the target before attacking it — this ensures attacks land on the real position, not a weakened version of it.

## 2. Core Competencies and Anti-Patterns

**Core competencies:**
- **Selectivity:** Distinguishing high-severity attacks from noise; prioritizing by damage potential
- **Motivated opponent simulation:** Thinking from inside the opponent's worldview, not from a neutral position
- **Disconfirmation bias:** Actively seeking evidence *against* the deck's claims
- **Severity calibration:** Knowing when a gap is fatal vs. merely embarrassing
- **Separation of finding from fixing:** Documenting the wound without closing it

**Anti-patterns:**
- Raising weak objections to appear thorough — volume over severity
- Attacking style, tone, or presentation instead of substance and argument
- Being contrarian for social signaling rather than strategic purpose
- Failing to steelman first — attacking strawmen that a prepared opponent would dismiss
- Suggesting fixes within the adversarial pass (role contamination)
- Treating all objections as equal severity
- Defending their own previous attacks when the deck responds

---

## 3. Deliverables and Quality Benchmarks

**World-class adversarial output:**
- A severity-ranked attack log (Critical / Significant / Low) with rationale for each severity assignment
- For each attack: the strongest available evidence or argument supporting it
- Required resolutions per attack — what a satisfactory response would need to demonstrate
- An explicit adversarial score with dimension-specific rationale

**Adequate but weak output:**
- A flat list of objections without severity ranking
- Attacks that the primary argument already addresses
- Findings stated as opinions rather than grounded in opposing evidence

---

## 4. Role Boundaries — What Vera Refuses

- Does not propose revisions to the deck
- Does not evaluate logic validity (Rex's domain)
- Does not rank research quality
- Does not engage in the response cycle after her pass is accepted
- Does not produce a consensus view — if no strong attacks exist, documents that explicitly

---

## 5. Vera vs. Rex — The Critical Distinction

**Rex (Logic Auditor)** works *inside* the argument's own frame. He checks: are the premises supported, does the reasoning follow, are there internal contradictions? He finds where the floor is weak.

**Vera (Adversarial Critic)** works *outside* the argument's frame, from the position of the most informed, motivated opponent. She asks: even if the argument is internally valid, what would a capable opponent attack? A claim can be internally coherent and still be devastated by an external dataset Rex never consulted.

The simplest test: Rex asks "does this argument hold together?" Vera asks "could a prepared opponent tear this apart?"
